Introduction & literature review

In this project, I would like to analysis the change of NBA play style between 2011 to 2020 years by looking at the difference of stats that players produce on court. Due to the big variation of player’s stats between playoff and regular season, especially for star players, I would only consider regular season stats in this project. The performance of NBA players across different positions can be compared to each other in the same standard with the same data set. Unlike the NFL, a player’s performance across different positions is measured across different categories of stats.

I didn’t find a paper that uses the same data as I do, but there are many of them which use similar data or write with similar purpose as I do. In the paper “Predicting NBA Player Performance” (1), the author used a more advanced machine learning algorithm to predict the performance of individual NBA players. The author mentioned the extension of predicting the outcomes of games by predicting the scoring performances of each player and summing them up. Therefore, the prediction of an individual’s performance can also contribute to the win-loss classifier. In the results of this paper, the author found out that the standard deviation of player’s performance is usually big due to injury and coach strategies which are all unpredictable but always affect the player’s performance a lot. The author also mentioned another important factor, the confidence level of a player. Such confidence level of a player is always determined by not only how well he himself but also his teammates or opponents play in the game, and his confidence level could affect his performance. But the author has not yet found a way to present a player’s confidence level into the regression model, since such confidence level is not numeric and does not have a standard to measure. In another paper, “Trends in NBA and Euroleague basketball: Analysis and comparison of statistical data from 2000 to 2017”, the author compares NBA and Euroleague basketball by the box-score statistics. They found out that the differences in win-loss determined factors are very small. From both leagues, game results are normally determined by four factors that could explain the most of the variation in their game results. Considering the biggest difference between Euroleague and NBA, they mentioned the “game pace”, where in NBA there are more numbers of possessions per game. In particular, the numbers of blocks per game, fouls per game, and number of free throws per game (free throws are usually caused by opponent’s fouls) in NBA games are higher than in Euroleague. This can be possibly explained by the fact that NBA players have better athleticism and Euroleague puts more emphasis on plays, and tactical aspects of basketball.

Such results from previous study are helpful to my project. As known the fact that players’ performance always variates a lot, I will not only consider points they made in the game as the only standard to measure their performance, but I will also include other stats such as: assists, rebounds, steals, blocks, turnovers into the measurements of their on-court performance. Besides, from the second article that contrasts the NBA and Euroleague, the author mentioned game pace difference and compare difference in athleticism between NBA and Euro players which can explain most of the variation between NBA and Euroleague. With that in mind, and knowing NBA has more stretch Four, Stretch Five in nowadays game than before, if I want to compare difference in play style with the athleticism of players does not change a lot, I should take a look into other angles such as 3-pointers per game, assists per game to analysis the change of the game.

Graphical and Statistical methods

In this project, I will perform an exploratory analysis of trends by plotting the variables over time. In each scatter plots, I will use the mean value of different variables over time to show the trend. For example, if I get a plot of average turnovers per game and observe a line with positive slope (in an increasing trend), that means that NBA players tent to make more and more turnovers in today’s game than the past. This outcome might not only caused by one factor, but a net outcome that affected by multiple factors, I will analysis each trend and give my interpretation based on each result. In addition, I want to create model to predict a player’s scoring ability based on other variables. To do that I will first show the correlation table between all the variables and the scatter plots between each variables and “p/g” (average points per game), so that I can decide what kind of regression model I would like to use, and what variables I would like to add to the model, and check if transformation is necessary to the model.

Before all of that, I will do some modification on the data, so that I can make my later process easier. The initial data I got have 16 variables in total, I first deleted some of them and remain the variables that I think can present the player’s performance more directly. And then I put 0 value to the missing data to make it in good integrity. I also make 9 data frame from the initial data to get the mean value of each variables with corresponding Year, so that it is convenient for me to create plots about those values later on.

Findings

Visulization and Graphs

Before presenting the graphs, I would like to present the correlationship between each variables and interpret some of them that might support my later interpretation of the graphs. In the correlation chart we can see that points (p/g) and turnovers (to/g), points (p/g) and threes (3/g) has a high positive relationship (0.72 and 0.47 respectively) which means a player tend to score more points in a game usually might also turn the ball over more times, and a player who can make more threes can also likely to score more points. These positive relationship between them are reasonable, since player who can score more usually touches ball more, and if they have the ball in their hand longer than other players, the chance to turn the ball over for them is also higher. For the positive relationship between threes and points is also straightforward, if you can make 3 points per shot rather than 2 points per shot, you can easily score more points. However, it is more difficult to make a 3 point shot than 2 point shot, so the relationship between points per game and 3 pointer per game is not that high (only 0.44). Rebound (r/g) and blocks (b/g) also has a high positive relationship (0.62). This also makes a lot of sense, since taller players are more likely to protect the rebounds and they are also easier to block other players as they are taller.

Now we can take a look at the graphs. In the graph of “p/g”, points score per game by a NBA player, we can see a clearly positive line that shows NBA players tend to score more points from 2011 to 2020. Meanwhile, similar positive line shows in the graph of “3/g”, this can easily related to the positive relationship between “p/g’ and”3/g”, as more three pointers are made in the game, average points a player scores and the total scores are all going to increase. However, as another variable that has a strong positive relationship with the “p/g”, “to/g” doesn’t have the same graph, it goes up and down every year since 2011, and we can conclude a kind of negative trend is there more the turnovers per game. Similar to “to/g”, “s/g” (steals per game) has almost the same graph with “to/g”, except the year of 2019. Recall the correlationship chart, we can observe a positive relationship (0.468) between these two variables as well. In real life, such correlation between turnovers and steals are also reasonable. Players get one turnovers when they lose the control of the ball, sometimes the ball goes out of the bound and switch the possessions of the ball or it falls to an opponents player’s hand, and that count as a steal for that opponent player, that why the positive relationship makes sense. Although not all turnovers will result in a steal but many of them did. In the graph of “a/g” (assist per game) and “ft%”, the trend are surprising similar to each other, worth to mentioned that the year of 2015 has the lowest in assist, free-throw percentage, and highest in blocks. These strange result can only be explained by in the data of 2015, it included more C and PF players (who are usually good and blocking, but bad in assists and ft%). Besides, the negative relationship between blocks and free-throw percentage (-0.42) can also prove this interpretation. Considering the changes in playing style of NBA players in general, the biggest difference is that they likely to make more three-pointers. And the NBA game is more pleasant to watch nowadays as an audience than the past: you can see they make more shots in a higher field goal percentage, and they are not likely to turn the ball over so that the game has a better fluency than before, they play more like a team, and try more harder to share the ball with their teammate on court. Those are all the improvement in NBA, improvement in modern Basketball.
